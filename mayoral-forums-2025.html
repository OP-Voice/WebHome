Shared Topics and Comparable Positions

1. Housing and the Comprehensive Plan (Framework OP)

Mayor Curt Skoog

Implement Framework OP; update the development code to allow more duplexes/townhomes/condos; target "all types and price points." (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Supports apartments where already zoned/approved; opposes up-zoning near single-family for higher density; prefers incentives alternatives; argues current pipeline already meets demand. (reporting)

2. Tax Incentives / TIF Policy

Mayor Curt Skoog

Defends incentive use for infrastructure and reinvestment; references OP's project-by-project evaluation and "pay-as-you-grow," with reporting available to the public. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Says incentives are excessive and opaque; would curb or eliminate most; proposes plain-English annual accounting of all subsidies and outcomes. (reporting)

3. Public Safety / ICE Cooperation

Mayor Curt Skoog

States OP does not participate in ICE operations and has not entered a 287(g) agreement. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Supports cooperation with federal immigration enforcement consistent with state law. (reporting)

4. Homelessness / Low-Barrier Housing

Mayor Curt Skoog

Open to partnerships with providers; references Johnson County efforts and existing city emergency shelter capacity. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

"Humane and firm" approachâ€”support charities, remove encampments from parks/public spaces; connect people with services while protecting parks. (reporting)

5. Economic Development / College Boulevard Corridor

Mayor Curt Skoog

Reimagine College Boulevard with a modern employment/residential mix of uses to maintain the city's economic engine. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Prioritize core services and fiscal tightening; skeptical of large civic or hotel-style public projects. (reporting)

6. Royals Stadium Proposal

Mayor Curt Skoog

Open to evaluating any state-led Overland Park proposal; would weigh incentives and community fit. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Opposes a stadium in Overland Park, citing crime/traffic/policing impacts. (reporting)

7. Diversity and Inclusion

Mayor Curt Skoog

Positions OP as a welcoming city, including LGBTQ+ residents, aligning partnerships and policies accordingly. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Emphasizes merit-based hiring; opposes DEI/CRT as screening criteria; supports education investment for opportunity. (reporting)

8. Community Amenities / Pools

Mayor Curt Skoog

Stated that neighborhood pools have high capital costs and that policy has tilted toward regional facilities; review underway. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Favors restoring neighborhood pools where feasible; questions the city's cost estimates; "keep promises." (reporting)

9. Budget & Taxes

Mayor Curt Skoog

Stated support for the current budget process and maintaining service levels and fiscal stability; described revenue-neutrality trade-offs. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Stated support for zero-based budgeting, forming a citizen advisory budget commission, and pursuing revenue neutrality / a property tax freeze. (reporting)

10. Campaign Finance / Conflict of Interest

Mayor Curt Skoog

Referenced current city campaign-finance and conflict-of-interest standards; did not outline new changes beyond existing practice in the forum. (reporting)

Dr. Faris Farassati

Proposed a recusal rule for council members receiving over $500 from an applicant and the creation of a permanent, public online voting database. (reporting)

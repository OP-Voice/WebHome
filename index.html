Our Mission: A Platform for Community Engagement

We build a more engaged and collaborative city by ensuring the people who power our economy have a meaningful voice in its future.

The Gap We Fill

Overland Park has channels for civic participation like the Chamber of Commerce and homeowners' associations. While valuable, these avenues often require a time commitment that many in our community workforce—retail workers, teachers, healthcare staff, and renters—simply don't have.

Overland Park Voice fills this gap. We make participation more accessible and impactful, organizing our community to focus on clear, common-sense goals. We are an additive force for a more inclusive civic dialogue.

Leveraging FrameworkOP

In May 2024, the City of Overland Park adopted FrameworkOP, its official comprehensive plan. This document provides a clear roadmap for the city's future. We use FrameworkOP as a foundational guide, aligning our efforts with the city's own stated goals to ensure our advocacy is focused, constructive, and effective.

Our Core Principles

These values guide everything we do.

Collaborative

We seek to build bridges, not walls. We believe the best outcomes are found when everyone works together.

Accessible

We work to demystify the civic process and lower the barriers to participation for every resident.

Data-Driven

We advocate for policies based on transparent data, paired with powerful, personal stories.

Results-Oriented

We focus on achievable goals that deliver a real return on the community's investment of time and effort.

Our Strategic Pillars

Our initial campaigns for an accessible government.

Goal: To ensure everyone who works in Overland Park can also afford to live here.

  • Activate the Overland Park Land Bank for Neighborhood Preservation.
  • Establish a Renters' Bill of Rights for safety, transparency, and stability.
  • Champion "Missing Middle" housing choices by participating in UDO updates.

Goal: To ensure public investments in development benefit the entire community.

  • Advocate for a clear, public rubric for evaluating projects requesting public incentives.
  • Promote Community Benefit Agreements (CBAs) to give the community a direct voice.

Goal: To directly lower the barriers to civic participation by equipping residents with knowledge and tools.

  • Create "The People's Leadership Program" (PLP), a flexible civic incubator.
  • Encourage broad-based community service on city boards and commissions.

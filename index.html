A Framework for Success

To ensure reforms are successful and accountable, we recommend piloting them in Strategic Investment Areas with progress tracked on a public dashboard.

The bottom line: We're not asking the City to adopt a new vision. We're providing a straightforward path to deliver the one it has already endorsed.

Key Questions Answered

Will this change neighborhood character?

Character comes from people. Allowing variety helps seniors downsize locally, families find starter homes, and teachers/nurses live where they work - strengthening character.

Won't parking changes create problems?

This provides flexibility, not bans. Forcing arbitrary parking requirements adds costs. Property owners can build what's actually needed.

Why allow businesses in residential areas?

Small-scale, neighborhood-serving shops create "15-minute neighborhoods" where you can walk for milk instead of drive everywhere.

Are we losing public input?

"By-right" development still meets all safety and design standards - it just streamlines the process for needed housing types.
